// Capstone Project · Sep 2025 – Jan 2026
Sustainable Air Conditioning System

Designed, built, and tested a regenerative indirect evaporative cooling (RIEC) system using a four-stage metal plate heat exchanger. The prototype successfully cooled air from 36.98°C to 24.70°C while keeping humidity reasonable, proving its potential as a low-energy AC alternative.

Bicycle Wrench Simulation

Performed a static structural ANSYS finite element analysis (FEA) of a 2 mm stainless-steel bicycle wrench under a 50 N hand load. Determined maximum stress concentration, von Mises stress, and structural deformation under fixed boundary conditions.

Designed a complete hydronic heating system for a building in Las Vegas using DesignBuilder for precise heat-loss estimation. Sized emitters, pipes, expansion vessel, and pump for the required 18.54 kW load, selecting a matching 20 kW boiler.
